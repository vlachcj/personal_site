There's nothing like ultimate.

I am currently in my 5th consecutive year with "Flywheel," the University of Michigan's Club Ultimate Frisbee team. Flywheel is a nationally competitive team that practices from September through the end of May. Since ultimate is not a varsity sport, we are the highest level of collegiate ultimate at the university. We train five to six days a week during our peak season and travel on weekends to other states, such as Georgia, Virginia, Texas, and California, to compete against other universities.

Playing a DI sport has driven me to push my physical and mental limitations in ways that I never had before. Flywheel has given me lasting relationships that can only come from complete trust and dedication to a team. Beyond my role as an athlete and teammate, I served as the treasurer for the club during my junior and senior years and am the current club president and team captain. The experience has given me exposure to all levels of involvement in a team and I am learning invaluable lessons in leadership on a daily basis.
